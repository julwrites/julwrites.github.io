How Timing Your Meals Affects Your Health

Key Metrics

Eating Window Duration

The number of hours per day you consume all your calories. Shorter windows (8-10 hours) are linked to better health outcomes.

Time of First Meal

When you start your eating window. Aligning this with your body's natural cortisol rhythm (higher in the morning) can improve hormone balance.

Daily Caloric Intake

The total number of calories you consume. While timing is crucial, caloric balance still drives weight loss or gain.

Health Indicators

Weight/Fat Loss Potential

Moderate

This indicator estimates your potential for weight and fat loss based on your eating window, caloric intake, and meal timing. A shorter window and a caloric deficit will increase your potential.

Hormone & Mood Health

Stable

This indicator reflects how your eating schedule aligns with your body's natural hormone rhythms. Eating earlier in the day and avoiding large meals before bed can improve hormone balance and mood.

Organ Health

Good

This indicator shows how your eating pattern affects your organs, particularly your liver. A longer fasting period gives your organs a chance to rest and repair.
